Watch Out for Homicides

2023
Xinyu Dong
Interactive Web Application
Overview

Compared to other high-income countries, the US has significantly greater homicide rates. Because homicides can cause significant negative effects, providing education about patterns and trends may help both everyday citizens and law enforcement agencies respond more effectively.

This project creates an interactive dashboard exploring homicide data across the United States from 2010 to 2014, enabling users to investigate how criminal activity is distributed geographically and over time.

Dashboard

Tab 1 — Geographic Distribution

The first tab visualizes the geographic distribution of homicides across the US. Users can filter by date range and weapon category. A choropleth map shows how cases are distributed geographically, while a bar chart summarizes aggregation by race and gender.

Fig 1. — Geographic distribution tab with date and weapon filters.

Tab 2 — Seasonal Trends

The second tab examines whether homicide frequency is regulated by season or month. A dropdown menu allows users to filter by relationship type, weapon, crime type, and state, revealing temporal patterns in the data.

Tab 3 — Crime Corridors

The third tab visualizes crime corridors between different race and gender groups using a Sankey diagram. The area on each side represents the percentage of a group as perpetrator or victim, while the width of each connecting line indicates the count of cases between groups.
